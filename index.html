What else can I know about Madagascar?

  • Madagascar is Malagasy and the national language is Malagasy, its second language is French.
  • Most of its inhabitants have traditional beliefs, are Christians, or an amalgamation of both.
  • Madagascar belongs to the group of least developed countries, according to the United Nations.
  • Ecotourism and agriculture, along with increased investments in education, health, and private business, are key elements of Madagascar's development strategy.
¡Important...!

As for the name "Malagasy" with which its inhabitants are called, the term comes from the French, which they took from "Malagasy", which was the name given to the original inhabitants of the island.

What to visit in Madagascar?

Geography and Geology

Terraced rice paddies in Madagascar's central highlands give way to rainforest along the coast.

The grassy plains that dominate the western landscape are dotted with stony massifs (left), patches of deciduous forest and baobab (right), while the south is characterized by desert and thorny forests.

Santa Maria Island

  • Known locally as Nosy Boraha, it is an island off the east coast of Madagascar.
  • The island is organized as the city of Isla Santa María.
  • Pirate Cemetery on Santa María Island.
  • It is a tourist destination, it has an elongated shape 49 km long and less than 5 km wide.

...In the weather it should be highlighted..!

  • The combination of the southeast trade winds and the northwest monsoons produces a warm rainy season (November-April) with frequently destructive cyclones, and a relatively cooler dry season (May-October).
  • Rain clouds originating over the Indian Ocean discharge much of its moisture onto the east coast of the island; Heavy precipitation supports the area's rain forest ecosystem.
  • The central highlands are both drier and colder, while the west is even drier, and a semi-arid climate prevails in the south-western and southern interior of the island.

...Don't Forget to Visit...!
